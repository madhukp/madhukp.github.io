Parameters:

The main parameter in this narration is the slide number which is controlled by the slide-navigator tool to the center-right of the screen. The current slide is indicated by an enlarged circle in the slide-navigator. Within each slide there are parameters represented by click buttons, radio buttons and brush bar that control the current state of the visual. Example, in the 'Overview' bar chart, radio buttons on the top control how and what data points are displayed. In the line chart year-brush-bar at the bottom provides a zoom-in/out feature and controls the x-axis year scale for the chart. Click buttons in donut chart and bubble chart control the year and grouping parameters respectively. And finally radio buttons in line chart and donut chart control include/not-include parameter for data points.


Triggers:

There are several noticeable triggers in this presentation. Users can hover over each circlular buttons in the slide-navigator tool to know the title of the slide and click on it to jump to that particular slide. Within each slide, actions performed on click buttons, radio buttons or brush bar trigger certain events. Example, in the 'Overview' bar chart, based on the radio button that users select the y-axis is fluidly re-scaled and bars are rearranged or removed or added in the view. In the line chart, based on the year window selected using the year-brush-bar at the bottom, the x-axis is re-scaled and data points for that year are displayed. Events like clicking on buttons in donut chart and bubble chart triggers the change in year snapshot and grouping of data points respectively. Clicking on radio buttons in line chart and donut chart toggles the display of respective data points. In addition, the radio buttons in donut chart also triggers re-scaling of arc angles. And finally whenever users hover over any shape representing a data point, a tooltip display is triggered along with enhanced focus on the shape. The main purpose of using elements like click buttons and radio buttons for triggering events was that they are very common web elements and so users will be instinctively aware of their presence and usage. The fill colors of the buttons toggles based on selection for user awareness.

About this visualization


Objective:

The objective of this project is to provide a visual narration on "Road Accident Deaths in India" and help users find answers to questions like -
     •  What was the trend in death counts from road accidents over years?
     •  How did the numbers compare Gender wise? Did any gender dominate the death toll counts?
     •  Did any particular region or states consistently report higher cases over years?
     •  To what age groups did the victims belong to? Are numbers for any age group concering?
     •  Should traffic/licensing rules for any particular mode of transportation be relooked at?


Narrative Visualization Method:

This project was build using interactive slideshow hybrid structures model to provide a narrative story. The slides are presented horizontally and descriptive information and guidelines are provided in each slide to aid interested users to drill down or explore each scene further.


Scenes:

There are four scenes in this narration that are presented as individual slides. Below is a brief description of each and the chart type used to design them. To maintain visual consistency across scenes, a two column layout with one column for charts and another for descriptive information/guidelines was used for all slides. Without distracting users, the chart and descriptive columns are alternated (left/right) across scenes for better visual appeal. Also the style of elements like font, click buttons, radio buttons, labels, tooltips, etc. are maintained consistent across scenes.
     •  Overview: A Bar Chart on total road accident death counts.
     •  States Comparison: A Line Chart for state-wise comparison.
     •  Age Group: A Donut Chart presenting death toll numbers by age group.
     •  Mode of Transportation: A Bubble Chart grouping numbers by vehicle size and type.


Annotations:

Annotations are used in each scene to call out user attentions on certain data points that are high or interesting or concerning. In the 'Overview' bar chart the annotations indicating trends are realigned and updated based on the parameters users select. In the line chart the annotations are cleared when users try to zoom in on the year scale to explore further or when they unselect the data points to which annotation relates to.

Overview



Total Road Accident Deaths over Years


In this first visual, total number of road accidents deaths in India from the year 2001 to 2016 grouped by gender are presented using bar chart. We can notice that overall the numbers have only increased, particularly between the year 2004 and 2010 there is a slight steep increase in numbers (indicated by annotation) which makes sense as each year the overall population and vehicle numbers have only climbed and never dropped allowing for more accident cases. Contrary to the trend seen between year 2004 and 2010 there was a slight dip in numbers in the year 2013 (also indicated by annotation) compared to its previous year, which is a good sign indicating that even as the population and vehicles count increase it is still possible to control the accidents and bring it down. Another concerning point to notice here is that the numbers for male are shockingly high compared to female.

States Comparison



Region/State wise comparison of Road Accident Deaths


Now that we have seen the total numbers lets take a peak at how they distributed across different states or regions using this multiple line graph here. Some of the states have reported very low numbers and some very high, so to better fit all states in this graph and for easy comparison logarithmic y-axis scale is used. We can see that some of the states have consitently reported high numbers, are traffic rules or licensing rules lax in these states and needs attention?



What you can do?


•  Drag and select on either end of the year-brush-bar at the bottom to
   select a particular window. This acts as a zoom in/out feature.
•  After selecting a year window you can also click and drag it left and right
   to shift the line chart focus area.
•  Click on radio buttons to include or not include particular regions in the
   view. Using this you can view trend for any one particular region or
   compare selective regions.

Age Group



Road Accident Deaths breakup by Age Group


In this vizualization road accident deaths are compared by age groups for 2014, 2015 and 2016 individually using donut chart. Here we can see that the numbers for teens/kids were significant in the year 2016, which is very troubling. This raises questions like - are safety rules for under aged kids like baby seats, seat belts, etc not followed properly that we are seeing such high numbers for them? Also age group "26 to 35" consistently took the major share in all three years. Should more awareness campaigns on traffic safety be held targetting this age group?



What you can do?


•  Click on buttons labeled with year to get a snapshop of the numbers for that
   particular year.
•  Click on radio buttons to include or not include particular age groups in the view.
   Using this you can compare selective age groups for a given year.
•  Hover over any arcs to view tooltips indicating actual numbers.


Mode of Transportation



Transportation mode and vehicle size


And finally the bubble chart here compares for the year 2016 the vehicles that were involved in the road accident deaths. Each bubble represents a transport mode and it's area represent the number of accident deaths that were observed with it. We can immediately notice that the numbers for Motor Cycles and Trucks are astonishingly high. This raises questions like - are current licensing procedures for these vehicles not stringent enough? Should special traffic rules be enfored for these modes?



What you can do?


•  Click on "All Transport Modes" button to view all the data points
   (bubbles) together.
•  Click on "By Transport Mode Size" button to view the data points
   (bubbles) grouped by vehicle size.
•  Hover over any bubble to view tooltips indicating actual numbers.
